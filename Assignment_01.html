Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The missile man of India

Here is a year-wise breakdown of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's journey from birth to death, highlighting his major achievements:

  • 1931: Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.

  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.

  • 1955: Enrolled in the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) to study Aerospace Engineering.

  • 1960: Completed his engineering studies and joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist.

  • 1969: Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and became the project director of India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980.

  • 1970-1990: Played a significant role in developing the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and Agni and Prithvi missiles under the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP). This earned him the nickname “Missile Man of India.”

  • 1981: Awarded the Padma Bhushan, one of India’s highest civilian honors.

  • 1990: Honored with the Padma Vibhushan for his exceptional and distinguished contribution to aerospace and defense research.

  • 1992-1999: Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of DRDO, making key contributions to India’s nuclear tests in 1998 (Pokhran-II).

  • 1997:Received India’s highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, for his contributions to science and technology.

  • 1998: Played a pivotal role in India's successful nuclear tests at Pokhran-II, which established India as a nuclear-armed state.

  • 2002: Elected as the 11th President of India and served from 2002 to 2007, becoming a beloved figure for his humility, vision, and efforts to inspire young Indians.

  • 2007:Completed his term as President and continued to engage in teaching, writing, and public speaking, especially focusing on inspiring youth.

  • 2011: Published his autobiography,"Turning Points: A Journey Through Challenges," highlighting his post-presidency experiences.

  • 2012: Published "Target 3 Billion," co-authored with Srijan Pal Singh, discussing ways to uplift the rural population of the world.

  • 2015: On July 27, 2015, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) in Shillong due to cardiac arrest.

"Throughout his life, Dr. Abdul Kalam was not only known for his scientific achievements but also for his vision for India's future, focusing on youth empowerment and education. His legacy continues to inspire millions of people around the world."

- 11th Indian President
